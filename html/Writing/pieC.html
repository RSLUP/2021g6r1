Pie Chart



The pie chart depicts/represents the Foreign direct investment in Sri Lanka in 2010 in services, manufacturing, agriculture and infrastructure sectors according to the Central Bank report.

Where the investment in the year 2020 is concerned 59% of the total investment has been made on infrastructure development while investment in manufacturing has taken 31%. Again 9% of the total investment has been allocated on the expenses for services and only miniature 1% of the total investment has been made in the agriculture sector. When the different segment of the pie chart is compared against the amount that has been allocated for different sectors it is clear that almost 2/3 of the investment has been spent on infrastructure development in various development projects and only about 1/3 of the total investment has been made on the infrastructure sector.

This chart also makes it clear that Sri Lanka is still at the primary level of development because the expenditure on infrastructure development is twice as big as the expenditure on manufacturing. By and large it shows rather slow development on agriculture and much attention has been given to the infrastructure sector.
